Shu-Ha-Ri (еЃИз†ійЫҐ) is a Japanese concept describing the three stages of mastery in any disciplined practice. It originates in martial arts and tea ceremony, but its logic applies wherever structured learning gives way to fluent action:

- **Shu (еЃИ)**вАФ_Protect/Follow._ Learn by adhering to the form. The form is not optional; it encodes principles the learner cannot yet see.
